Story Hollow Site Plan

Ariana Jerome

Overview

Purpose

Every writer has a story inside of them, but sometimes they're unable to express that story in the ideal ways they have in their heads. That's why I want to create a website where publishing your short story is fun, easy, and professional. Wouldn't it be cool to be able to not only upload a short story you've written, but also illustrations and music/ambiance to go along with it? You can truly engage your audience with your work as they have these additions in mind.

Audience

The truth is, there's no age limit for creativity. Anyone that can have access to a computer or smart phone should easily be able to share their short stories online through Story Hollow, just as long as they're respectful, professional, and considerate of others. We also encourage users to not share stories of graphic or adult nature without a rating tag. That way, once readers go online, they'll know what kind of story they'll be in for.

The Basics

Storyhollow is a website that showcases your writing in new and experimentive ways. There are countless storytellers out there that have their tales written down, but they don’t quite know how to share it with the world. Of course, there are websites like Wattpad, AOL or Fanfiction.net, but those websites, especially Fanfiction.net, are usually used for, well, fanfiction. At storyhollow.com, we encourage users to write their own stories, with their own world-building and their own narratives and characters. We also encourage users to search for new stories and get inspired by the stories other people tell.

General

In terms of website design, Storyhollow will be like Tumblr, or Pinterest, where users have the freedom to make and link their own sub-blogs. If users want the full experience of Storyhollow, they’ll need to sign up for a Storyhollow account. Users can also easily search for usernames, or story titles in the search bar on the homepage. Finally, users can discover new content on the discovery page, where they’ll be able to search for new stories to read under genre tags. In these discover pages, the user will be shown the most popular stories as of right now. They could be short stories, or new chapters of a larger novel that come out weekly. When a user is recommended a story, they’ll usually be redirected to the beginning of the story, so that they can start fresh.

The Story Layout

When a user clicks on a story, they’ll be directed to the beginning of the story. The author of the story has the option to add pictures, ambient background music, color themes and fonts. This can allow for some truly creative investment coming from the user reading the story. The user also has the option to like and share the story, and subscribe to the author for more content. The author will be required to add genre and trigger warning tags so the reader knows what kind of story they’re in for. If the user feels as though the author gave a false tag, they can report the author, and depending on the severity of the false tag, the author could be forced to take their story down, or even permanently banned from Storyhollow. (Idea: before publishing their story, the author could be required to fill out a questionnaire about their story so that they’ll know the appropriate tags to add to it.)

Pictures

Pictures may add a bit of a visual element to a story. The limit will be 1 picture per 1,000 words. (Please keep in mind, this isn’t conventionally a webcomic platform, although if the website gets popular enough, we could introduce tags specifically for webcomics.) These pictures could be public domain photos, photography or even drawings that the author made themselves! Feel free to be creative!

Music

Authors are allowed to add some sort of continuous music track that goes along well with their story. Music can add a whole other layer of complexity to a story if executed well. Keep in mind, certain music can be more distracting than inviting, so make sure you choose your music wisely. If you do choose to add a music track, you will need to make sure it’s royalty free, or even music that you produced yourself.

Author's Page

Finally, this page will detail general information about the author. Please keep in mind, literally anyone could visit this site, so make sure you only share information you feel comfortable with strangers knowing, like your username, hobbies, favorite genres, past works, etc. It would not be wise to share revealing information about yourself. Be smart. Be safe.

Your author's page will showcase all of the works that you've published onto Storyhollow. Through your author's page, people can search for a specific story. Make sure you update often so that you can have a large audience!
